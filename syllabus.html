Bibliography

The course slides and tutorials will be made on the e-learning platform.

To go a bit more into the subject or to have something more structured, there are several open-source books and online resources that you can use as introductory materials to R programming. Here is a list of a few:

  • Introductory materials
    • Grolemund (2014) Hands-On Programming with R. O'Reilly. https://rstudio-education.github.io/hopr/
    • Peng  (2022) R Programming for Data Science. https://bookdown.org/rdpeng/rprogdatascience/
  • More advanced materials
    • Grolemund and Wickham (2017) R for Data Science (2e). O'Reilly. https://r4ds.had.co.nz/
    • Gagolewski (2024) Deep R Programming. https://deepr.gagolewski.com/
    • Wilke (2019) Fundamentals of Data Visualization. O'Reilly. https://clauswilke.com/dataviz/ 

Another very good, but non-open-source, book on R is

  • Adler (2012) R in a Nutshell (2e). O'Reilly.
